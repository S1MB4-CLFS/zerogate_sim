# DREED / active_inference_review_lens_v0

**Status:** public method note for ZeroGateSim v1.0-alpha

DREED means **Direct Review Epistemic Estimate Display**.

Inside ZeroGateSim, DREED is used as a review discipline: a way to expose where pressure concentrates before treating a result, claim, graph, manuscript section, or simulation output as meaningful.

DREED is not a judge.

DREED is not peer review.

DREED is not a truth machine.

DREED does not prove equations, validate experiments, predict acceptance, or simulate named reviewers.

Its role is narrower and safer:

- show where mechanism pressure appears;
- show where integration pressure appears;
- show where witness / translation pressure appears;
- identify the smallest useful repair;
- expose overreach risk before a claim becomes louder than its support.

## Product-safe identity

Use the public method name:

```text
active_inference_review_lens_v0
```

Use anonymous review forms only:

```text
mechanism_boundary
integration_modularity
witness_translation
```

Do not encode personal names, personal profiles, private inspirations, or claims about what a specific real person would say.

## Review forms

### mechanism_boundary

Mechanism-boundary review asks what the model, metric, or claim actually measures, what assumption carries the result, and what alternative process could produce the same visible output.

In ZeroGateSim, this protects against treating toy signal outputs as direct physical dimensions.

### integration_modularity

Integration-modularity review asks where a finding belongs, what action it enables, and whether it can be used without expanding the project into fog.

In ZeroGateSim, this protects against turning every interesting pressure into a new theory section or a new engine mutation.

### witness_translation

Witness-translation review asks what is actually justified, what must be bounded, and whether the intended reader can understand the claim without private architecture.

In ZeroGateSim, this protects against treating first-research-alpha results as cosmology, physics, or proof that reality is trinary.

## Support filters

### Trace

Can a serious reader follow the path?

This checks definitions, variables, equations, figures, report order, source references, and claim boundaries.

### Example-fit

Does the example carry the right mechanism?

This protects against examples that feel right but smuggle a different process than the claim actually requires.

### Repair-minimality

What is the smallest useful repair, and what is the overdo risk?

This protects against making the repair larger than the pressure.

## ZeroGateSim use

In ZeroGateSim, DREED supports the project rule:

```text
Evidence is what the simulation produced.
Logic is what follows from the model.
Inference is what may be suggested.
Metaphor is how we explain it.
Speculation is what remains unproven.
```

The first-research-alpha result is therefore bounded as software-theory evidence inside generated toy fields, not as proof of cosmology or physics.
