## Why `wide243` existed

The trinary weather ladder was not random.

```text
triad27 = 3^3
noise × relation × expansion

deep81 = 3^4
noise × relation × expansion × perturbation

wide243 = 3^5
noise × relation × expansion × perturbation × time
```

The `wide243` profile adds `time_axis`, which is the temporal-depth / temporal-cohesion stress. It asks whether the witness survives compressed, baseline, and stretched time pressure.

This is why the historical `wide243` run matters: it was not just a bigger number. It was the first broad stress form where temporal pressure could interact with noise, relation, expansion, and perturbation.

## What role-blind shadow design means

The current proof harness is role-aware. It can evaluate known roles such as:

```text
expresser
latent/probe
trap
```

Role-blind shadow design means adding a **non-authoritative shadow reader** that does not read those labels. It would estimate false-one risk from observable behavior only.

Candidate observable metrics:

```text
limiting gate
return gap
zero-depth instability
raw expression without lineage
echo mimic pressure
relation dependency
late collapse
threshold fragility
ablation crown risk
temporal-axis sensitivity
```

It is called a shadow because it should sit beside the current role-aware witness first. It should not replace the final witness and should not crown or demote candidates in production language until validated.

First scientific question for role-blind shadow:

```text
Can observable behavior rank trap-like or false-one-like pressure above earned expression
without reading the designed truth-role labels?
```

If yes, ZeroGateSim starts moving beyond harness validation. If no, the model remains useful as a controlled adversarial harness, but not yet as role-blind discovery.
