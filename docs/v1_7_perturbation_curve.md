## Curve law — spectrum, not scalar

The perturbation curve exists to expose behavior, not merely totals. Raw pressure may move under weather.

```text
raw pressure may move under adversarial weather;
final witness must fail safely;
+1 when earned;
0 when unresolved;
-1 when false.
```

A successful curve does not require every lane to stay constant. It requires the final witness to preserve the distinction between pressure and earned expression.

## Pass posture

| pressure behavior | acceptable final witness behavior |
|---|---|
| raw expression pressure rises | hold as raw pressure, debt, or demotion unless earned |
| relation debt rises | structured zero, not final +1 |
| return debt rises | structured zero, not zero-crossing theater |
| echo dependence rises | block or demote borrowed coherence |
| lineage instability rises | delay or demote one-frame expression |
| false-one pressure rises | visible `-1` / demotion, final false-one crowns remain zero |

## Fail posture

The spectrum fails if perturbation creates new-looking pressure and the witness crowns it without return, lineage, and independence.

The spectrum also fails if safety is achieved by refusing all earned-one. That is dead-safe, not intelligence.

## Boundary

`v1.7.4-alpha` adds no new heavy evidence crown. It defines the perturbation-spectrum witness surface that later evidence must satisfy.
